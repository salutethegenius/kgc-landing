The Founder's Desk
“I am not trying to recreate
Silicon Valley. I am building
Bahamian excellence.”

I grew up here. I understand both the talent and the gaps. After living abroad and seeing what strong infrastructure can do, I became convinced that The Bahamas does not lack ability. We need stronger digital systems, shared knowledge, and coordinated opportunity.

Artificial intelligence is not foreign. It is a tool. And if we participate in shaping it, we ensure it reflects our values, our laws, and our realities.

This work is not about control. It is about contribution. It is about building bridges so the next generation does not have to figure everything out alone.

The Vision — A Bahamas where:
  • 01 Government spending is transparent and searchable — Citizens can easily understand how public funds are allocated. BahamasOpenData makes this accessible.
  • 02 Students access world class digital education from anywhere in the country — Kemis Academy prepares Bahamians for careers in AI and technology, without requiring relocation.
  • 03 Entrepreneurs participate in global commerce — KemisPay expands digital payment access for freelancers and small businesses.
  • 04 Legal information is understandable — LawBey helps citizens explore Bahamian statutes and legal frameworks in plain language.
  • 05 Local developers build local systems — Talent stays, grows, and leads.

Why We Exist

Digital consumer.
Or digital owner.

Right now, most of the data, apps, and AI tools used in the Bahamas come from abroad. That means we don't own our future. We pay for infrastructure built for someone else's context. That ends here.

$0
retained locally for every dollar spent on foreign SaaS. The value leaves. The dependency stays.
100%
of Bahamian data should be owned by Bahamians. Not processed in data centres we don't control.
15
verticals built to replace foreign platform dependency with locally owned infrastructure. One at a time.
1st
in the Caribbean building an AI sovereign digital ecosystem. We are not following. We are leading.
Before KGC vs With KGC
Before
  • Data processed on foreign servers — you don't own it
  • Legal answers require expensive lawyers or foreign AI with no Bahamian context
  • Government budget data locked in PDFs no one can search
  • Freelancers can't get paid online without foreign bank accounts
  • AI tools built for the US/UK — applied poorly to local contexts
  • Tech talent trained here leaves — no local ecosystem to join
With KGC
  • VerityOS™ runs locally — your data never leaves the country
  • LawBey grounds every answer in actual Bahamian statutes with citations
  • BahamasOpenData makes every dollar of public spending traceable
  • KemisPay connects Bahamian freelancers to global payments directly
  • AI trained on Bahamian data for Bahamian context — accurate, local, owned
  • 15 growing products that need Bahamian engineers to build and maintain
